Populist Consensus

The real majority — the people who are the foundation of society, yet own very little of it — already agree on the issues that matter most; these problems are not left or right or Democrat or Republican:

  • Economic fairness & accountability
  • An affordable life
  • Punishing political corruption
  • Ending culture wars

Who is the real majority? It's not politicians, lawyers, infotainment personalities, bankers, billionaires, corporate executives, influencers, culture warriors, or celebrities. It's...

  • The people who feed — farmers, ranchers, processors, food workers, supply chain operators
  • The people who heal — nurses, caregivers, therapists, doctors, first responders
  • The people who power — utility workers, linemen, energy technicians, internet and telecom crews
  • The people who clean — sanitation workers, janitors, waste management, custodians
  • The people who move — truckers, delivery workers, transit operators, warehouse workers
  • The people who build — manufacturers, construction crews, mechanics, designers, programmers
  • The people who make — tradespeople, machinists, fabricators, skilled laborers, technicians, engineers
  • The people who serve — public servants, retail workers, gig labor, hospitality, service industry, administrators
  • The people who teach — educators, mentors, professors, childcare workers, trainers, tutors
  • The people who protect — military, law enforcement, firefighters, security, emergency responders

Economic fairness & accountability

The U.S. economy is built to keep wages low, prices high, and profits flowing upward. Companies cut jobs, suppress pay, and hike costs — not out of necessity, but because they can. Economic fairness means fair wages, profit-sharing, job security, and worker representation in the decisions that shape our livelihoods. We create the wealth and are owed a share in it. No more extraction or imbalance — just a fair deal for the people who keep this country running.

An affordable life

An affordable life means covering the basics — housing, food, healthcare, utilities, insurance, transportation, savings — without constant anxiety or stress. But the cost of living keeps rising while essentials become luxuries. This isn't just inflation or market forces; it's unregulated profit-driven exploitation of markets. Corporations, landlords, and insurers destroy competition and manipulate markets because there's no check on their greed. The bare minimum we expect: cost controls on housing and healthcare, tight regulation of markets, and policies that stop essential goods from being treated as high-margin commodities. People shouldn't have to go into debt just to live.

Punishing political corruption

Corruption isn't a flaw in the system — it is the system. Politicians take corporate money, pass laws that enrich their donors, and leave the public to deal with the consequences. They sell influence, trade stocks on insider knowledge, and walk away rich while the rest of us pay the price. Punishing political corruption means banning corporate donations, killing the SuperPAC, setting term limits, ending lobbying loopholes, and more. Illegally profit from public office? We seize their assets. Violated the law in office? We send them to prison. No more revolving doors. No more backroom deals. Serve the people or face the consequences.

Ending culture wars

No law can dictate your thoughts, and no regulation can shield you from being misled. The key to ending the culture wars lies in personal accountability, not merely policy adjustments. Every day, we're bombarded with messages urging us to see one another as enemies. Some media figure points at your neighbor and insists, "There's the enemy!" They thrive on your outrage, profit from conflict, maintain control through fear, and laugh as you waste energy on battles that lead nowhere. Ending the culture wars means not allowing these manipulators to dictate your thoughts and actions. If a billionaire on TikTok tries to incite you against someone who shares your struggles, question who really benefits from that narrative. Focus on what genuinely matters — your family, your community, and their future. Stand firm!
